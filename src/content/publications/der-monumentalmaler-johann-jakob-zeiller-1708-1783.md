      Johann Jakob Zeiller, the most significant scion of the Reutte painter
      family Zeiller, played a decisive role in the development of the late
      Baroque fresco.


      His most magnificent work is certainly the Ettal dome fresco. He worked in
      Ottobeuren for seven whole years. With his ideas implemented al fresco,
      this virtuoso of Baroque painting proved to be a congenial interpreter of
      the built architecture.


      Josef Mair, known for his excellent studies on Balthasar Riepp (2003) and
      Paul Zeiller (2008), has now also clearly outlined and explained Johann
      Jakob Zeiller's work, making it understandable even for the layperson.
