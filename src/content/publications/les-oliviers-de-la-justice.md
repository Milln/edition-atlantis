      Les Oliviers de la Justice (1959) sont un livre de tendresse et d’émotion,
      de colère parfois - mais surtout de reconnaissance, où sont peints les
      rapports réels – et souvent secrets et "souterrains" - entre Musulmans et
      Européens. Ainsi, à travers une expérience particulière, se dessine un
      tableau de l’âme et de la sensibilité algérienne.


      L’auteur a voulu faire chanter la vérité, celle des hommes – celle qui
      fait comprendre et aimer.


      C’est donc aussi, malgré tout, un livre d’espoir.

      Someone said we had to write a press release for the newspapers, and we
      wrote it on the balcony, using the customary words. "... He was one of
      those first-generation settlers who, through their labor, fertilized the

      Les Oliviers de la Justice (1959) is a book of tenderness and emotion,
      sometimes of anger – but above all of recognition, where the real – and
      often secret and "underground" – relationships between Muslims and
      Europeans are depicted. Thus, through a particular experience, a portrait
      of the Algerian soul and sensitivity is drawn.


      The author wanted to make the truth sing, that of men – the one that makes
      one understand and love.


      It is therefore, despite everything, a book of hope.
