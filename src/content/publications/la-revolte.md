      Das heimtückische Komplott, das hier ausgeheckt wird, sagte Samar, ist
      unser eigener Untergang. Man will uns ausliefern. Und nun verdichtet sich

      Der Feind mag schweigen. Er ist überall um diese Stadt herum, in der wir
      nun mit der Armee eingeschlossen sind. Er weiß, dass sich hier das

  en:
    text: >-
      The treacherous plot being prepared, said Samar, is our assassination.
      They want to hand us over. And now everything is coming to a head in one
      of those silences that, it is said, precede great furies.


      The enemy may be silent. He is everywhere around this city where we are
      now locked up with the army. He knows that here the fate will be decided.
      He is waiting. He probably cannot believe that the army will subscribe to
      the diabolical plan. He knows that his victory, the rush, the looting, and
      the carnage do not depend on his savagery or his fury, but on the decision
      the army will make.


      And we, we know that our life or our death, or our exile, which will be
      another form of death, also depend on the same decree.


      With the army we can do anything, because we remain citizens. Without it,
      we will only be mutineers.


      But we will assume our destiny as mutineers. We will fight. We have laid
      the foundations of a clandestine organization. It is still only a draft;
      but around it we will mobilize the city.


      In this masterpiece, Samar, this alter ego of Jean Brune, lives the final
      phase of the Algerian War in Algiers, where the OAS, the FLN, and the
      French Army clash in a merciless struggle.
