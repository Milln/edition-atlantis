    text: >-
      May 28, 1977, Hossegor: Maïsée, a young woman who has been psychologically
      fragile since her Algerian childhood, disappears mysteriously, leaving her
      sister Hélène and her husband Adrien, a former adventurer, in deep
      distress. One on the island of Noirmoutier, the other in the highlands of
      Hérault, they lock themselves in the past and regrets.


      In November 2007, Rachid the Algerian appears, leading them to a ghost
      village at the foot of the Mountain that sings where, for thirty years,
      another life has unfolded, populated by baroque characters: Karima, the
      murderess, Majdala, the proud Berber, Ali, the innocent, Baba Antoine, the
      fundamentalist, Qura, the voice of the Mountain, and others still.


      A novel crossed by unacknowledged and difficult loves, where "every day is
      a victory".


      Maïa Alonso lives in Gascony after a nomadic youth between Mascara,
      Toulouse, London, and Paris. She has published three novels: L'odyssée de
      Grain de Bled en terre d'Ifriqiya (L'Harmattan, 2013), Le soleil colonial
      – Au Royaume des cailloux (Atlantis, 2014; Prix Terre d'Eghriss 2014), and
      Les enfants de la Licorne (Atlantis, 2015).
